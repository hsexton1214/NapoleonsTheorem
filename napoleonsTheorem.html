The Median Concurrence Theorem (Centroids)

Theorem: The three medians of a triangle are
concurrent in a point called a centroid. 

                       ---:::---

This theorem dates back to Archimedes, where in
On the Equilibrium of Planes he states:

Proposition 13: “In any triangle the centre of gravity
lies on the straight line joining any angle to the middle
point of the opposite side.”
Proposition 14: “the centre of gravity of any triangle
is at the intersection of the lines drawn from any two
angles to the middle points of the opposite sides respectively.”
